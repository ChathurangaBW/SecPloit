# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our standards

Participation in SecPloit should be professional, evidence-based, and respectful. Constructive technical disagreement is welcome. Harassment, threats, discriminatory conduct, doxxing, and publication of private credentials or undisclosed vulnerability details are not acceptable.

## Security-research conduct

Contributors must:

- work only on systems they own or are explicitly authorized to assess;
- respect scope, rate, data-handling, and disclosure requirements;
- avoid destructive testing, persistence, denial of service, and credential reuse outside the authorized range;
- remove secrets and sensitive target data from issues, pull requests, logs, and test artifacts;
- use private security advisories for suspected vulnerabilities in SecPloit itself.

## Enforcement

Repository maintainers may edit or remove contributions and restrict participation when behavior or content violates these standards. Serious security or safety concerns should be reported privately through GitHub's security-advisory mechanism.
